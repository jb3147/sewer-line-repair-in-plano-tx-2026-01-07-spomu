Common Sewer Problems in Frisco

Frisco's unique soil composition and climate create specific challenges for sewer systems. Here's what we commonly encounter:

Tree Root Intrusion

Frisco's mature landscaping means tree roots seeking moisture frequently invade sewer lines, causing blockages and structural damage.

Clay Soil Settling

Frisco's clay-heavy soil can shift and settle over time, causing sewer pipes to crack, collapse, or misalign at joints.

Aging Pipes

Older clay and cast iron pipes in established Frisco neighborhoods deteriorate over time, leading to corrosion and breaks.

Blockages

Grease buildup, debris, and improper flushing create stubborn blockages that back up into your home or yard.

Slow Drains

Multiple fixtures draining slowly throughout your home often indicates a sewer line issue rather than individual drain problems.

Foul Odors

Persistent sewage smells in your yard or near drains indicate cracks or breaks in your sewer line allowing gas escape.
