Common McKinney Sewer Problems

McKinney's clay soil, mature trees, and older infrastructure create specific challenges. Here's what we commonly encounter and fix.

1

Tree Root Intrusion

McKinney's mature trees thrive in our soil, but roots seek moisture from sewer lines. We identify and remove roots while preventing recurrence with specialized treatments.

2

Clay Soil Settlement

Our heavy clay soil shifts with moisture changes, causing pipes to crack or settle unevenly. We repair damage and recommend stabilization methods to prevent future issues.

3

Pipe Corrosion

Older clay and cast iron pipes corrode over time, creating blockages and leaks. We assess pipe condition and recommend replacement or lining solutions.

4

Grease & Debris Buildup

Grease, soap, and debris accumulate in pipes, causing slow drains and backups. We use hydro-jetting to clear blockages and restore full flow capacity.

Why Trenchless Repair Wins

Minimal Disruption

No need to excavate your entire yard. We access pipes through existing cleanouts, preserving your landscaping, driveway, and hardscaping.

Cost Savings

Lower labor costs and no restoration expenses mean trenchless repair often costs less than traditional digging, even for extensive damage.

Faster Completion

Most trenchless repairs complete in 1-2 days versus weeks for traditional excavation. Get your sewer system working again quickly.

Long-Lasting Results

Epoxy pipe lining creates a new pipe within your existing pipe, lasting 50+ years with minimal maintenance required.
